### Distributions 
<img width="1005" height="365" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b0e6a1e1-aace-45e4-9f5f-346cecd2db98" />
A histogram can only show what actually happened in the sample — a distribution curve estimates the probability of what could happen, including values we have never observed. One looks backward, the other looks forward.
